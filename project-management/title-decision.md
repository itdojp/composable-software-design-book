# Title Decision

## Candidate Evaluation

| Candidate | Clarity | Searchability | Technical accuracy | Distinctiveness | Scope fit | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Compositional Software Design for AI Agents | 4 | 5 | 3 | 4 | 3 | Searchable, but it can imply that AI agents are the audience rather than the system context. |
| Composable Software Design for Agentic Systems | 4 | 4 | 3 | 4 | 4 | Accessible wording, but less precise than compositional in a category-theoretic setting. |
| Compositional Software Design for Agentic Systems | 5 | 4 | 5 | 4 | 5 | Balanced, technically aligned, and broad enough for design artifacts, diagrams, and case studies. |
| Category-Theoretic Software Design for AI Agents | 3 | 4 | 4 | 4 | 3 | Accurate, but too math-forward for the practical engineering emphasis of the book. |
| Designing Verifiable Agentic Systems with Composition | 3 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 4 | Distinctive, but weaker as a primary title and less searchable. |
| Composition, Diagrams, and AI-Assisted Engineering | 3 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 4 | Strong subtitle material, but not strong enough as the main title. |

## Adopted Title Set

- Title: Compositional Software Design for Agentic Systems
- Subtitle: A Category-Theoretic Guide to Human-AI Boundaries and Verifiable Engineering
- One-line description: A practical guide to designing AI-assisted software systems with composition, diagrams, and effect boundaries that remain auditable and verifiable.

## Adoption Rationale

This title keeps the core search terms `compositional`, `software design`, and `agentic systems`.
It matches the planned scope across responsibility boundaries, formal structure, and AI-assisted implementation.
It is technically more accurate than `for AI Agents`, which can suggest AI agents are the intended readers.
The subtitle adds the formal and governance dimensions without overloading the primary title.
A repository slug rename is not required at this stage, and any GitHub-side rename can remain a separate manual decision.
